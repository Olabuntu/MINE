Welcome to Buntu Blog 🧬

On Computational Genomics, Mechanistic Biology, and the Genotype–Phenotype Problem

👋 This blog documents my journey into computational genomics and the pursuit of a deeper question:

How does genetic variation shape complex phenotypes? 🤔

Modern biology generates massive amounts of genomic and transcriptomic data — SNP arrays, RNA-seq profiles, GWAS, QTL mapping outputs, regulatory sequence data. Yet translating these datasets into mechanistic understanding remains one of the central challenges in biology. 📊

My work sits at the intersection of genomics, bioinformatics, artificial intelligence, and molecular biology. Through this blog, I explore:

  • 🧬 Genome-wide association studies (GWAS) and statistical genetics
  • 📈 RNA-seq and gene expression modeling
  • 🔍 Variant interpretation and SNP analysis
  • 🤖 Deep learning for biological sequences
  • ⚙️ Integrative computational pipelines for genomic research

But this space is not just about methods.

It is about interpretation. 💡

Prediction alone is not enough. Models that achieve high accuracy without biological insight offer limited value. My long-term goal is to build computational systems that move beyond correlation toward mechanistic explanation — systems that help us understand how genotype influences phenotype through regulatory networks, molecular pathways, and cellular processes. 🧪

📝 Along the way, I will share:

  • 🔧 Technical deep-dives into genomic workflows
  • 💭 Reflections on research projects and lessons learned
  • ✅ Practical approaches to building reproducible computational systems
  • 🎓 Insights for students transitioning from wet-lab biology into computational genomics

🌍 I am especially motivated to make computational genomics more accessible to researchers in resource-limited environments, particularly across Africa, where high-impact biological questions intersect with limited infrastructure.

This blog is a record of that process — learning, building, questioning, refining.
