About Me

Who I Am

I am a computational genomics researcher in training, driven by a deep interest in understanding how genetic variation shapes complex phenotypes. My long-term goal is to uncover the molecular mechanisms that connect genotype to phenotype using integrative computational genomics approaches.

My work sits at the intersection of genomics, artificial intelligence, and molecular biology. I build data-driven systems that analyze high-dimensional biological data — including SNPs, RNA-seq expression profiles, GWAS datasets, and genomic selection models — to extract mechanistic insight from complex biological systems.

Currently, I work as a Graduate Research Intern at the International Institute of Tropical Agriculture (IITA), where I contribute to genome-wide association studies, genomic selection modeling, RNA-seq analysis pipelines, and SNP database engineering to accelerate trait discovery and breeding efficiency. My research combines statistical genetics, machine learning, and deep learning to identify candidate genes, interpret variant effects, and model genotype–trait relationships.

Previously, I developed AI-guided pipelines for drug discovery, integrating experimental assays with machine learning, molecular docking, and predictive modeling. These experiences strengthened my ability to bridge wet-lab molecular biology with computational modeling — a foundation that now fuels my interest in mechanistic genomics.

I am particularly interested in:

  • Mechanistic interpretation of genotype-to-phenotype relationships
  • Integrative analysis of GWAS and transcriptomic datasets
  • Variant effect prediction (SNPs and regulatory sequences)
  • Gene regulatory modeling and promoter analysis
  • AI-driven discovery in genomics and precision biology

My Mission: To develop interpretable computational systems that move beyond prediction — toward biological understanding.
