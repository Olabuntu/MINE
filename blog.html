Notes on Computational Genomics & Mechanistic Biology

This is where I think in public.

I write about computational genomics, bioinformatics, AI-driven biological modeling, and the challenge of interpreting how genetic variation shapes complex phenotypes. These essays explore research ideas, methodological insights, technical breakdowns, and reflections from my journey into mechanistic genotype–phenotype modeling.

From GWAS and RNA-seq analysis to deep learning for biological sequences, this space documents the process of building interpretable systems for understanding biology, and not just predicting outcomes.
